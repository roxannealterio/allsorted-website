Meet the founder

Why I built
All Sorted.

Roxanne Alterio · Founder

I built All Sorted because I was a mess. Not in a dramatic way. Just in the quiet, exhausting way that most people are. Bills I forgot to pay. Work trips I never logged. A tax return that was always less than it should have been because I could not remember what I spent money on six months ago.

I had six different apps. A notes app for reminders. A spreadsheet for bills. A separate logbook for work trips. Another app for budgeting that I opened twice and abandoned. None of them talked to each other. None of them understood that all of this stuff is connected.

I did not need more apps. I needed one app that actually understood how life works.

The moment it clicked

I was doing my tax return. My accountant asked for my work trip logbook. I had it, kind of, in a notes app. Some trips were there. Most were not. I had rough numbers in my head. She looked at me and said, “You’re probably missing thousands of dollars in deductions.”

That was the moment. Not because of the money, though that stung. But because I realised I had been making my life harder than it needed to be, for years, because the tools I was using were not built for the way I actually live.

What I wanted to build

I wanted something that felt calm, not stressful. Something beautiful enough that you actually want to open it. Something that understood that a work trip and a tax deduction are the same thing, and that paying a bill and flagging it for the ATO should be one tap, not a trip between three different apps.

I wanted a morning briefing that felt like a PA had already looked at your day. Bills due today. A note to keep you going. I wanted BPAY built right in so you never have to open your banking app just to pay a bill. I wanted to import a bill from an email by pasting the text, without re-typing a single number. I wanted to scan a receipt with my camera and have it automatically flagged as a work expense with a single Yes. I wanted to invite a friend to an event without leaving the app, and have it check whether they are already on All Sorted before sending.

I wanted passwords that stay on your device only, encrypted by iOS, never uploaded anywhere. I wanted a grocery list that lives next to your to dos, not in a completely separate app. I wanted tasks that carry forward every day until you actually tick them off, because that is how real life works. Done tasks should stay crossed out all day so you can see what you got done, then clear overnight so tomorrow is a fresh start.

I wanted the Life tab to be genuinely useful. Your full calendar right at the top, with strip, week and month views. Invite a friend to an event without leaving the app. Your to do list and grocery list below the calendar so your whole day is in one scroll. Health rings that sync automatically from Apple Health, seven-day charts for water, steps and sleep, and activity logging with one tap.

I wanted four colour themes because your phone is personal. Classic, Blush, Denim and Sage. The backgrounds always stay warm cream. Only the accents change. And I wanted your home screen completely customisable, because what matters to me is not what matters to you.

I wanted to build the app I had been looking for for years and could never find.

What ended up in it

Drive is the hero. Turn on Auto-Detect once and every work trip logs itself. Stop moving and $0.92/km for FY 2025–26 lands in your ATO total before you even put your keys down. The Logbook tab shows every trip with route map and purpose. Reports gives you your financial year summary, monthly breakdown bar charts and a built-in ATO Logbook Compliance check. Team lets small business owners track multiple drivers from one place. Export the full logbook as a CSV for your accountant in one tap.

Tax lives on your home screen. A clean widget shows your estimated refund, a progress bar and total deductions logged this year. Every receipt scanned and marked Yes, every flagged bill, every kilometre driven — they all feed into one running total that updates live throughout the day. Tap the widget for the full breakdown. Export everything to your accountant in one tap.

Bills has BPAY built in. Scan a bill with your camera and details fill automatically. Or paste your bill email and the app reads the amount, due date and reference on-device — nothing sent to any server. Receipts has a spending chart by category. Your calendar lives inside Life. Incomplete tasks carry over. Ticked tasks stay crossed out all day then clear overnight. Because ignoring something does not make it go away, and actually finishing things feels really good.

Where we are now

All Sorted is launching in June 2026 on iOS. We are in TestFlight right now, which means real people are using it on real phones every day. Drive with GPS auto-detection and logbook compliance check. Bills with BPAY and email import. Receipts and Tax that talk to each other. The full calendar inside Life. Health with Apple Health sync. The customisable home screen with a live tax refund widget. All of it working.

The founding member waitlist is open now, and the response has been more than I expected. People are tired of the chaos. They just want to be sorted.

If that sounds like you, I would love to have you in from the beginning.
