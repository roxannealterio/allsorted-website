The backstory

I've spent years working across health, fitness and real estate — two worlds that couldn't be more different on the surface, but share one thing: they demand everything from you, all at once.

"I understood early on that how you organise your life directly affects how you show up in it."

In health and fitness, I saw how the most successful people weren't just the most motivated — they were the most consistent. And consistency comes from having systems. Reminders for your workouts, your check-ins, your goals. Everything accounted for so nothing falls through.

In real estate, I learned that on top of client meetings, contracts and deadlines, there's a whole invisible layer — bills, car rego, tax deductions, work trips — that piles up when you're not looking.

I was juggling five different apps, still missing things. Nothing felt aesthetic enough that I actually wanted to open it. Nothing felt calm.

"I built All Sorted because I needed it. And I built it to feel aesthetic because I believe you're more likely to use something when it looks and feels good."

Who it's for

All Sorted isn't just for real estate agents or gym junkies. It's for anyone with a life that moves fast — the professional who needs to track work expenses, the person who keeps forgetting to pay their bills on time, the one who could claim more at tax time but never gets around to logging receipts.

It's for the person who wants to feel calm and in control — without adding more to their plate.

Why it matters

Most people are leaving money on the table every single year. Not because they're not working hard enough — but because the tools to track it are scattered, ugly and easy to ignore. All Sorted fixes that.

One app. Everything you forget, in one aesthetic place.
