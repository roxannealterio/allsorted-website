Meet the founder

Why I built
All Sorted.

Roxanne Alterio, Founder

I built All Sorted because I was a mess. Not in a dramatic way. Just in the quiet, exhausting way that most people are. Bills I forgot to pay. Work trips I never logged. A tax return that was always less than it should have been because I couldn't remember what I spent money on six months ago.

I had six different apps. A notes app for reminders. A spreadsheet for bills. A separate logbook for work trips. Another app for budgeting that I opened twice and abandoned. None of them talked to each other. None of them understood that all of this stuff is connected.

I didn't need more apps. I needed one app that actually understood how life works.

The moment it clicked

I was doing my tax return. My accountant asked for my work trip logbook. I had it, kind of, in a notes app. Some trips were there. Most weren't. I had rough numbers in my head. She looked at me and said, "You're probably missing thousands of dollars in deductions."

That was the moment. Not because of the money, though that stung. But because I realised I had been making my life harder than it needed to be, for years, because the tools I was using weren't built for the way I actually live.

What I wanted to build

I wanted something that felt calm, not stressful. Something beautiful enough that you actually want to open it. Something that understood that a work trip and a tax deduction are the same thing, and that paying a bill and flagging it for the ATO should be one tap, not a trip between three different apps.

I wanted a morning briefing that felt like a PA had already looked at your day. Bills due today. Reminders. A note to keep you going. I wanted BPAY built right in so you never have to open your banking app just to pay a bill. I wanted to scan a receipt with your camera and have it automatically flagged as a work expense. I wanted to invite a friend to an event without leaving the app, and have it check whether they are already on All Sorted before sending.

I wanted passwords that stay on your device only, encrypted by iOS, never uploaded anywhere. I wanted savings goals that actually feel achievable. I wanted a grocery list that lives next to your to dos, not in a completely separate app. I wanted tasks that carry forward every day until you actually tick them off, because that is how real life works. Done tasks should stay crossed out on screen all day so you can see what you got done, then clear overnight so tomorrow is a fresh start.

I wanted the Life tab to be genuinely useful. Not just a step counter. A Health tab with rings synced from Apple Health and daily targets. A Wellness tab with a mood and energy check-in that gives you a real personalised insight, not a generic tip. A journal. Reflect, meditate and manifest. And a Daily tab for your to dos, grocery list and journal, all in one place.

I wanted four colour themes because your phone is personal. Classic, Blush, Denim and Sage. The backgrounds always stay warm cream. Only the accents change. And I wanted your home screen completely customisable, because what matters to me is not what matters to you.

I wanted to build the app I had been looking for for years and could never find.

What ended up in it

Seven tabs. Every one connected to the others. A work trip flagged in Drive appears instantly as an ATO deduction in Money. A bill paid in Bills can be flagged as a deduction in one tap. Your grocery list lives in the Daily tab next to your to dos and journal. Your calendar invites go directly to friends already on the app, or include a download link for those who are not.

The invoice category is in there. BPAY biller codes. A receipt scanner that reads the text off your receipt and pre-fills the details. Eight colours for your calendar events so you can tell them apart at a glance. A How to Use guide built into Settings so nothing feels confusing on day one.

And the thing I am most proud of: incomplete tasks carry over until you actually do them. Ticked tasks stay crossed out all day so you can see what you got done, then clear overnight. Because ignoring something doesn't make it go away, and actually finishing things feels really good.

Where we are now

All Sorted is launching in June 2026 on iOS. We are in TestFlight right now, which means real people are using it on real phones every day. The Calendar, Bills with BPAY, Drive with GPS auto-detection, the Life tab with Health, Wellness and Daily, the receipt scanner, savings goals, the customisable home screen. All of it working.

The founding member waitlist is open now, and the response has been more than I expected. People are tired of the chaos. They just want to be sorted.

If that sounds like you, I would love to have you in from the beginning.

Roxanne Alterio
Founder, All Sorted
